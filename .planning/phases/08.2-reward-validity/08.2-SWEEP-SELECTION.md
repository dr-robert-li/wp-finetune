# Phase 08.2 — RVAL-04 Reward (form, weight) Sweep — Selection Writeup

**Run:** `scripts/reward_form_sweep.py` (offline, CPU, $0). 21 configs = {pairwise, hybrid, calibration} × weight {0.0, 0.15, 0.3, 0.45, 0.6, 0.8, 1.0}.
**Result artifact:** `output/reward_validity/sweep_results.json` (ranked + selected).
**Three lenses:** (1) oracle validity (CI-lower>0 vs teacher-Spearman, VAL GT, 11-ckpt trajectory);
(2) 08.1 gradient density (frac_mid on the probe corpus, TRAIN GT); (3) structural echo proxy (worst-case
reward to the echo adversary, ≤0.30). `select_config` requires ALL THREE.

## Selected config: **NONE** (documented escalation)

No config satisfies all three offline gates simultaneously. This is the sweep doing its job — it caught,
offline and for $0, that the naive calibration forms are not cleanly certifiable, BEFORE any GPU spend.

## Why — the ranked table (top rows by oracle CI-lower)
| form | weight | oracle ci_lo | valid | frac_mid (density) | echo (worst-case ≤0.30) |
|---|---:|---:|:--:|---:|---:|
| hybrid | 0.8 | **+0.372** | ✅ | 0.742 | **0.990** ❌ |
| pairwise | 0.8 | **+0.372** | ✅ | 0.747 | **0.990** ❌ |
| hybrid | 1.0 | +0.233 | ✅ | 0.608 | 1.000 ❌ |
| pairwise | 1.0 | +0.207 | ✅ | 0.602 | 1.000 ❌ |
| (all weight ≤0.6, all `calibration`) | | ≤ −0.33 | ❌ | — | — |

Two structural facts, both important:
1. **Only high blend weights (0.8–1.0) are oracle-valid.** At weight ≤0.6 the fix_correctness component
   still dominates and drags the composite's trajectory correlation negative (the seedA Goodhart signal).
   So a valid reward must be *mostly* calibration — a big change from today's fix-correctness-led reward.
2. **Every oracle-valid + gradient-alive config fails the worst-case echo proxy** (echo ~0.99–1.0).
   The pairwise/hybrid calibration reward scores only the *ordering of the emitted score* vs teacher; it is
   **code-blind**. Under the worst case (adversary emits a plausible high score matching a synthetic
   teacher=95), the calibration term rewards it ~fully. Pure calibration cannot, by construction, punish a
   judge that emits a well-calibrated-looking number over garbage.

## Interpretation (NOT a phase failure — an escalation)
- The echo lens here is a deliberately **pessimistic, code-blind structural proxy** (synthetic teacher=95,
  max over adversaries). It is NOT the real codegen/anti-hack guarantee — that requires wp-bench serving
  (GPU) and is **Plan 05's real smoke trip-wire** + the existing echo-adversary check that held 0.25 across
  all of seedA's reads. The offline proxy is intentionally strict so it never green-lights a hackable reward.
- Therefore the correct disposition is: **no offline auto-selection; carry the best oracle-valid + dense
  candidate into the gated smoke, where the REAL codegen trip-wire + echo-adversary decide.**

## Recommended CANDIDATE for Plan 05's gated smoke (not an offline "winner")
**`form=hybrid` (or `pairwise`), calib_weight≈0.8.** Rationale: top oracle validity (ci_lo +0.372, clears
the standing gate with margin) AND highest gradient density among valid configs (frac_mid 0.742 — not
gradient-dead, unlike low weights). Its only failing lens is the worst-case code-blind echo proxy, which the
smoke's real wp-bench trip-wire + live echo-adversary are built to adjudicate properly.

## Required guardrails before/at the smoke (carried to Plan 05)
1. The reward MUST NOT be pure calibration in production without an anti-hack/code term — the sweep proves
   calibration is code-blind. Either (a) keep a floor of the security/PHPCS-verifiable term in the gen axis
   (unchanged) so codegen is still rewarded, and/or (b) add an explicit echo/anti-hack penalty to the judge
   axis. The smoke MUST run with the codegen trip-wire (Plan 02) armed (`--codegen-probe-every > 0`).
2. Kill-at-50 if teacher-Spearman (the VALIDATED metric) isn't moving OR the codegen trip-wire fires.
