<objective>
JUDGE-03 (the crux) — measure judge rho on the 3 relabel-SFT seeds against the held-out relabeled val, on
BOTH measurement methodologies, and compare CI-aware against the pre-registered targets (>0.85 single-seed OR
>0.87 3-seed median ensemble vs the 0.8075 v3.0 shipping wall).

Two methodologies, explicit about which gates what (per 21-RESEARCH.md):
- CHEAP (promotion/iteration): Tinker-capture (`capture_judge_responses_tinker.py`, --max-tokens 8192) ->
  `eval_relabel.py` Spearman rho per seed + 3-seed median ensemble. No merge, no GB10. This is how v1.3's
  promotion decision (0.8274 single-seed) was actually made.
- LITERAL (the requirement text): merge the promoted seed -> vLLM-serve at 8192-token cap -> `eval_relabel.py`
  final rho. GB10. This is the shipping-figure methodology (v3.0's 0.8075 was produced this way at packaging).

Wave-4 placement: depends on 21-03 (the 3 seed checkpoints, data dependency) AND 21-05 (GB10 resource
serialization — 21-05 holds the sole 67 GiB residency in Wave 3; this plan's final merge+serve must not
co-reside, so it is bumped to Wave 4; the cheap Tinker-capture sub-steps are remote/CPU and parallel-safe).

The vLLM-served 3-MERGED-checkpoint ensemble (median of 3 served models) is deferred to packaging (Phase 27),
consistent with v1.3/Phase 15 — this plan PRESERVES all 3 seed checkpoints for it. The cheap-path ensemble
median IS computed here (it needs only the 3 captures, no merges).

Output: a cheap-path rho receipt (`judge03_capture_rho.json`) + the final JUDGE-03 receipt (`judge03_rho.json`).
</objective>
