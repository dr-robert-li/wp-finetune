## What the bisect conclusively establishes

1. Format collapse is present from the earliest checkpoint and *decreases* monotonically with
   training (65%→37%), plateauing far above the 10% bar — it is not a late-training artifact.
2. The collapsed outputs are **not** the trained target schema. They are the **base model's
   native judge format** (bare JSON; `security_score / accessibility_score /
   documentation_score / must_fix_issues`; 0–100 scale) — the merged model is reverting to
   the base prior, not reproducing a contaminated target. The trained target is
   `[/REASONING]` + `<judge_output>{9-dim, 0–9/overall≈74}`.
3. Mechanism (best supported): the LoRA format-imprint is too weak to override the base prior,
   despite 100%-clean targets.

## Unresolved confound (why "additional iteration", not a Path)

Both bisect arms share the **same MoE merge** (attention-only PEFT + per-expert MoE delta).
The bisect controls the *adapter* — so the 28-point *difference* is training-attributable —
but cannot separate whether the *baseline* collapse is **training-under-imprint** vs
**merge-under-imprint** of the format-control weights. The required discriminator —
unmerged adapter-on-base (PEFT) inference on the identical slice — was **not** part of
Steps 0–4. Until it runs, choosing Path-A or Path-B would be addressing a non-cause.

## Recommended next diagnostic (for the follow-up plan)

Before any GPU retrain: run the identical cot+ctf @2048 slice through **unmerged
adapter-on-base** PEFT inference for ckpt-72 (and optionally ckpt-50), measure the same
`[/REASONING]` terse rate, and compare to the merged 37% / 65%:
- **Unmerged clean (≪10%) but merged collapses** → merge artifact: fix/strengthen the

- **Unmerged also collapses (≈ merged)** → training under-imprint: strengthen the format
  signal (LoRA rank/targets, format-reinforcing objective) — a *new* corrective branch, not
  the tabled Path-A or Path-B.
