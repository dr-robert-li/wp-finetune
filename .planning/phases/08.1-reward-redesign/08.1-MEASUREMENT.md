## Pathology Diagnosis

Mapped through RESEARCH.md "Two-Pathology Decision Criteria" table:

### Evidence

| Signal | Observed | Threshold | Interpretation |
|--------|----------|-----------|----------------|
| parse-failure rate | 68.01% | >40% | Parse-cliff 0-mode active |
| rubric.overall spread on parseable | mean=98.9, std=4.4, 96.6% in [90,100] | clusters >80 | Lax-checks >90 cluster confirmed |
| frac_mid (judge) | 0.011 | — | Effectively binary judge reward |
| frac_mid (gen) | 0.202 | — | Gen-path is graded, not saturated |
| frac_groups_all_zero (judge) | 0.312 | >0.50 | Below group-collapse threshold — Lever 3 not primary |
| frac_groups_all_zero (gen) | 0.323 | >0.50 | Below group-collapse threshold |

### Diagnosis: BOTH PATHOLOGIES COEXIST

The judge-path `fix_correctness` is effectively binary through two simultaneous mechanisms:

**Pathology A — Parse-cliff 0-mode:** 68% of completions do not contain a parseable PHP block, yielding `fix_correctness=0.0` via the `else: 0.0` hard gate (`rl_rollouts.py:632`). This creates an artificial 0-mode that is not a genuine quality signal — these are completions that attempted a fix but produced a non-parseable PHP block, or prose-only outputs.

**Pathology B — Lax-checks >90 cluster:** Of the 32% that ARE parseable, 96.6% score in the [90,100] decile (mean=98.9, std=4.4). The verifiable checks in `_extract_verifiable_signals` are too permissive — they pass too easily, providing no discrimination on the parseable subset. frac_mid=0.011 on the entire judge distribution (all 372 samples) confirms: only 1.1% of scores fall in the mid-range (0.1, 0.9) that would drive gradient.

**Per D-81-04 — Judge path is the saturated path:** judge frac_mid=0.011 ≪ gen frac_mid=0.202. The gen-path composite is already graded (VeRPO / MO-GRPO machinery, partial credit in `reward_pipeline.py`). The judge-path is the primary source of reward collapse. Leave gen-path untouched; touch only judge-path per D-81-04.

**Lever 3 (group-collapse) status:** frac_groups_all_zero=0.312 is below the 0.50 group-collapse threshold from RESEARCH Q4. Group collapse is NOT the primary mechanism — the judge reward is already non-uniform at the group level (frac_groups_nonuniform=0.677) because parse-failures mix with parseable successes within groups. Lever 3 per-group advantage shaping is a structural backstop, not the root fix.

## Selected Lever(s)

### Selected lever: Lever 1 Form A + Lever 2 (weight rebalance backstop)

**Lever 1 Form A (Primary fix — de-saturate the parse cliff):** Replace the `else: fix_score = 0.0` hard gate with partial credit for non-empty but unparseable corrected blocks. The 68.01% parse-failure rate is the dominant mechanism suppressing mid-range rewards; addressing it directly would shift ~half of all samples from 0.0 to a small-but-nonzero value, immediately expanding frac_mid above the 0 baseline. This is the minimum intervention that addresses the largest single contributor to reward collapse.

**Lever 2 (Backstop — weight rebalance toward consistency):** The consistency term already yields frac_mid=0.73 (per Phase 9 handoff, Section H), meaning it is graded where fix_correctness is not. Shifting weight from the saturated 0.7 fix_correctness anchor toward the 0.3 consistency term (within the existing guard at `rl_rollouts.py:173`, cap 0.5) amplifies a signal that is already discriminating, as a backstop in case Lever 1 alone leaves the >90 cluster problem unresolved.

**Why NOT Lever 1 Form B (sub-check fraction / graded within parseable):** The parseable subset is already at mean=98.9/std=4.4 — the sub-checks in `rubric.overall` are saturated. Reweighting saturated sub-checks cannot manufacture spread that does not exist in the underlying signals. Form B is the correct fix for a world where `rubric.overall` is spread 10-90 (graded rubric, parse-cliff creates the 0-mode); it is not the correct fix for a world where `rubric.overall` clusters >90 (lax checks). The RESEARCH.md prohibition exists precisely because these two worlds need different fixes; the measurement confirms the lax-checks world. (If Plan 03 wishes to confirm at the sub-check level, a sub-check breakdown histogram could be emitted before considering Form B — this is a forward pointer, not a requirement here.)

**Why NOT Lever 4 (harder/finer checks) as primary:** Lever 4 is the direct fix for the lax-checks Pathology B, but it requires identifying and implementing new PHP/WP-specific checks in `_extract_verifiable_signals` — higher implementation risk and longer feedback loop. It is the correct eventual fix, but Lever 2 weight rebalance achieves a similar effect (amplifying the graded consistency signal) with lower blast radius and zero new check-design risk. Lever 4 is deferred to Plan 03 as an optional enhancement — if sub-check inspection reveals a clear set of failing checks to add, it should be included; if not, Lever 2 alone is sufficient.

**Why NOT Lever 3 as primary:** frac_groups_all_zero=0.312 is below the 0.50 group-collapse threshold; groups are already structurally non-uniform. Lever 3 per-group advantage shaping addresses groups that are uniformly scored — that is not the primary failure mode here. It can be added as a structural backstop if Lever 1+2 still show high frac_groups_all_zero after the short signal-check, but it should not be the lead intervention.

**Rationale tied to measured numbers:** The measured frac_mid=0.011 on the judge path (vs 0.202 on gen) is the key discriminating number (D-81-04). A successful redesign must move judge frac_mid above 0 — the ROADMAP SC1 gate. Lever 1 Form A directly addresses the 68% parse-failure mass that contributes to frac_mid=0. Lever 2 raises the weight of the consistency term (which is graded at frac_mid~0.73) as a backstop against the >90 cluster. Together they address both pathologies with minimal blast radius to Phase 8's verified guarantees.
