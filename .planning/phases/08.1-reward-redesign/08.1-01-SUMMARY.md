## Decisions Made

**1. BOTH pathologies coexist — judge path effectively binary**
Parse-failure rate 68.01% (parse-cliff 0-mode) AND parseable scores clustered at mean=98.9/std=4.4 (lax-checks >90 cluster). frac_mid=0.011 confirms near-zero mid-range scores on the judge path.

**2. Judge path is the saturated path per D-81-04**
Judge frac_mid=0.011 vs gen frac_mid=0.202. Gen-path is graded (VeRPO/MO-GRPO). Touch only judge-path.

**3. Selected lever: Lever 1 Form A + Lever 2**
Lever 1 Form A (partial credit for non-empty unparseable blocks) addresses the dominant 68% parse-failure mass. Lever 2 (shift weight toward graded consistency term, within existing `combine_judge_reward` guard) backstops the >90 cluster saturation by amplifying the graded consistency signal (frac_mid≈0.73 per handoff). Form B rejected (saturated sub-checks cannot manufacture spread). Lever 3 not primary (frac_groups_all_zero=0.312 < 0.50 threshold). Lever 4 deferred (valid eventual fix for lax-checks, but Lever 2 achieves similar effect with lower risk).

**4. Corpus label: primary (not failure-biased)**
The `--completions` path prints a generic "failure-biased" warning; this corpus is the fresh primary measurement corpus (not the failure-biased on-disk cross-check). Documented in MEASUREMENT.md.
