## HARD Gate Failure Analysis

### REVL-01A: Spearman 0.240 < 0.263 (FAIL)

The merged endpoint Spearman dropped from the pre-merge Tinker-sampler rho of 0.294 to 0.240 post-merge. This is below the D-V4-04 do-no-harm floor of 0.263 (v3 baseline floor). The merge process degraded the judge-axis correlation. 

- n_pairs: 120, p_value: 0.008 (statistically significant)
- revl01b teacher-Spearman (SOFT): 0.504 — model still tracks teacher verdicts reasonably
- Interpretation: calibrated_canonical GT correlation degraded post-merge, suggesting the merge affected the model's ability to match the rubric-calibrated canonical scores

### REVL-02: PHPCS 0.9412 < 0.98 (FAIL)

1 of 17 generation samples failed PHPCS (phpcs_pass_rate=0.9412). The 2pp tolerance bar is 0.98 (baseline 1.0 − 0.02). This is a fresh measurement on the v4-winner merged endpoint from Plan 02 eval_gen_results.json — the prior 1.0 reading was from the dead v4-nolmhead model and does not carry forward per the plan's re-measure requirement.

- security_pass_rate: 0.8889 (informational, not separately gated)
- This is the codegen risk axis the iteration-2 thesis named; the real failure here confirms the thesis was correct to flag REVL-02 as HARD

### Confusion Pareto: false-FAIL 0.468 > 0.403 (FAIL)

The merged endpoint over-fails teacher-PASS cases: 29 of 62 teacher-PASS rows get effective FAIL verdict (false-FAIL rate 0.468). The P4 Pareto bar is 0.403 (from VERDICT-POLICY.md / run_grid_eval contract). Recall on teacher-FAIL is strong (0.845 > 0.638), but the Pareto joint condition requires BOTH axes to pass.

- Over-strictness is directionally consistent with the REVL-01A finding (the model is more punitive post-merge)
- REVL-07 (SOFT, F1=0.721) corroborates: good recall, moderate precision, over-strict on PASS cases
