# Feature Research — v4.0 Rerun on Qwen/Qwen3.6-35B-A3B

**Domain:** Base-model capability re-verification for a locked pipeline rerun (not a new feature landscape)
**Researched:** 2026-07-12
**Confidence:** MEDIUM (vendor claims independently corroborated on architecture/infra facts; coding-quality claims contested by informal community re-tests; no rigorous third-party numeric reproduction of the two headline benchmarks found)

This is not a generic "what exists in this domain" survey. `PIPELINE.md` and all pipeline stages are already built (out of scope). The only open question is: **what does `Qwen/Qwen3.6-35B-A3B`'s verified capability profile imply for the expected outcome of each already-defined rerun stage?** Findings below re-verify the six claims from `.planning/V4-RERUN-ROADMAP.md` (locked 2026-07-11) against sources as of 2026-07-12, then translate each into a stage-level expectation.

## 3. Table Stakes vs Differentiators (for this rerun's requirements)

**Table stakes (expect these to just work, low research risk):**
- eos/pad token alignment fix (Stage 1.5) — documented, mechanical, one-time.
- Text-only serving via `--language-model-only` (VL merge/serve path) — vendor-documented, field-proven on GB10.
- aarch64/DGX-Spark op compatibility — already field-proven by multiple independent deployments.
- Quantization/GGUF packaging ecosystem — mature, multiple independent conversions already published for this exact model.
- Gen-side SFT clearing the wp-bench floor — gen was already near-saturated on the weaker old base; a stronger base should not regress this.

**Uncertain / genuinely open (flag for requirements + hands-on verification, not assumption):**
- Whether the always-on-by-default thinking mode requires an explicit SFT data-format decision before Stage 2/3 (new finding, not in current roadmap).
- Whether Stage 3's judge output stays parseable given community-reported 18% format-noncompliance and identifier-hallucination signals — this is the single most decision-relevant new finding, since parse-rate was the exact failure mode that eliminated 3/4 ratios on the old base.
- Whether the vendor's 73.4/80.4 benchmark ceiling is reachable on the WP-specific structured-output task at all, given the unofficial-scaffold caveat and the informal community regression report.
- LoRA-adapter merge reliability for this MoE family under Transformers 5.x (new finding) — recommend explicitly smoke-testing merge success as part of the already-planned Phase 20 VL merge-path check, rather than assuming Stage 2's "should transfer directly" framing covers it.
- Gate B/C expert-redundancy headroom — modestly more plausible than before (checkpoints exist) but still numerically unsubstantiated; keep the roadmap's existing calibration, do not treat as pre-validated.

**Anti-features / do-not-assume (things this research explicitly refutes or fails to substantiate):**
- Do NOT assume the vendor's 73.4 SWE-bench Verified figure is a standard-harness, independently-reproduced number — it is not (measured on Alibaba's internal scaffold; only informal, and lower, community numbers exist for a related setup).
- Do NOT treat the REAP "competitive" claim on the 20%-drop community checkpoint as benchmark evidence — no scores are published on the checkpoint card itself.
- Do NOT generalize the Qwen3.5-specific LoRA rank/LR guidance found in search as Qwen3.6-verified — it's adjacent-model analogy, not a direct report, though the "don't train the router" convention is well-corroborated and does match the pipeline's own D-09-08 decision.
